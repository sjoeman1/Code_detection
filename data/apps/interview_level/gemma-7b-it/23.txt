```python
a, b = map(int, input().split())

# Convert a to a list of digits
a_digits = [int(x) for x in str(a)]

# Sort the digits in descending order
a_digits.sort()

# Construct the maximum number not exceeding b
max_num = 0
for i in a_digits:
    max_num = max_num * 10 + i

# Remove leading zeroes
max_num = str(max_num).lstrip('0') or '0'

# Print the maximum number
print(max_num)
```

**Explanation:**

1. Convert the integer `a` to a list of digits.
2. Sort the digits in descending order.
3. Construct the maximum number not exceeding `b` by multiplying each digit by the appropriate power of 10 and adding it to the maximum number.
4. Remove leading zeroes from the maximum number.
5. Print the maximum number.
